About simulation-based modelling

We are in the age of machine learning. Machine learning models are changing the world. Their accurate predictions allow for the automation of many new tasks.

You don't however want to automate decision making. Instead you’d want to inform decision making with insights gained from data. Due to their black-box nature, it is not possible to gain insight with machine learning models. Statistical models and traditional-AI models are also not ideal.

Simulation models on the other hand are ideally suited for gaining insights from data. The goal here is to create a virtual twin of any real system. I.e. you create a simulation which behaves in the exact same way as the real world. These models are therefore very intuitive. You can watch the behavior of the system, understand the mechanisms and identify key components. You can also investigate what-if questions by re-running the simulation with some detail changed.

About Tree of Knowledge


Uploaded data is integrated into a global schema and is used for model training & evaluation. The simulation models can build uppon each other.
There already exist a range of simulation modelling tools ( Anylogic, Simio, etc.). They are commonly used for optimizing processes in a range of fields such as manufacturing plants, hospitals, etc. They haven’t however caught on in many other fields, I believe there are two reasons for this:
  • the correctness of the models is not guaranteed, as they are often not thoroughly evaluated
  • it takes a long time to create a model

Tree of Knowledge solves these problems. In fact, Tree of Knowledge’s ability to solve these problems increases over time….
  • data which is uploaded with a free account is added to an integrated data repository. This well documented data is then used to automatically evaluate simulation models, guaranteeing its correctness.
  • Objects’ behaviours that have been proven to apply in some model are remembered and tested in future models. Making it possible to only describe the objects in the system and having the objects’ behaviours automatically inferred, thereby drastically reducing the development time.

This is all possible due to the nature of Tree of Knowledge, which is not only a simulation tool, but also a universal knowledge repository. Over time it builds up a complete understanding of the objects in this world, their relations, behaviours and their states at different times in the past.

Additionally, Tree of Knowledge allows for the easy use of fairly advanced functionality, such as:
  • Optimizing systems to maximize KPIs
  • Learning model parameters with Bayesian inference
  • Flexible and powerful simulation visualization
  • Collaborative model creation

Our Philiosophy

As mentioned, by being used, Tree of Knowledge automatically builds up an increasing knowledge of the objects in the world, their behavior and their past states. The long-term goal of Tree of Knowledge is to become a repository of the worlds’ knowledge. Making the knowledge accessible in a computer-readable and highly useable form.
There will always be free public APIs for accessing its data and simulation rules.

Tree of Knowledge is non-profit and is dedicated to improving the world as much as possible - NGOs and well-meaning governments will always have free access. The access for harmful companies is restricted.
